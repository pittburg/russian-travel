Travel Across Russia

The real country isn't on the news. It's here.

What haven't we seen there yet?

According to a survey by VTsIOM, 95% of Russians dream of travelling somewhere, but only 36% plan to spend their vacation in their home country. Why bother, they say — we've already seen it all at home. In reality, Russia is an entire universe, from the warm southern seas to the dense forests of the Sayan Mountains and the harsh ice of the Putorana Plateau. And you don't need millions in the bank, a foreign passport, or hours-long flights to see all this beauty. Take Vera Bashmakova, for example — a brave young mother who packed her three kids into her Lada and drove 20,000 kilometers across her home country. We've picked out and described a few places worth your next vacation.

Curonian Spit

url

park-kosa.ru

Here, among forests and sand dunes, you can see two different bodies of water — the calm Curonian Lagoon on one side and the rippling Baltic Sea on the other. A unique natural area on the edge of Russia's exclave.

Kaliningrad region doesn't end there. Nearby, travelers and explorers will find Russia's westernmost point, the Baltic Spit, along with the German heritage scattered across small seaside towns. The atmosphere here leaves no room for rush, immersing you in calm nature and the smell of the cold, steely sea.

Kola Peninsula

url

National Geographic

Almost the entire peninsula lies beyond the Arctic Circle. Sami tundra gives way to taiga in the south, and to the Arctic Ocean — disguised as the Barents Sea — in the north.

You may have seen Zvyagintsev's films or heard about the Arctic festival in Teriberka. Perhaps the word "Khibiny" hasn't faded from your school geography lessons. Maybe you've never thought about the superdeep borehole piercing the Earth's crust, or felt indifferent toward apatite ore. But your dream of seeing the northern lights starts coming true with a ticket to Murmansk.

Altai

url

Facebook

Altai is one of the most beautiful places in Russia, largely because of its mountains: drive along the ridge and you'll see slopes covered in pines, mountain rivers and lakes. Roll down the car windows and you'll discover the area's invisible wonder — its mountain air.

Altai has a temperate climate, so summer is the best time to visit. That's when you'll see the full diversity of local flora and fauna. Elk wander through Altai's forests, eagles soar above the ridges, and roe deer graze on the plains. The famous Pallas's cats also call the Altai region home.

Winter Baikal

url

https://vk.com/baikalmile

Everyone knows Lake Baikal as the largest lake in the world. Many also know it's the largest source of fresh water and one of the most beautiful places in Russia.

All of that is true, of course. But Baikal is also the perfect place for skikjoring competitions — a sport where a skier is towed by a motorcycle, and the pair tries to reach as high a speed as possible on the ice. In March 2019, a world record of 197.011 km/h was set at the Baikal Mile festival.

Karelia

url

http://vodlozero.ru/

Siberia doesn't end at the Urals — it ends in Karelia: the Siberian larch that forms the taiga doesn't grow west of Lake Vodlozero. But here it grows up to 30 meters tall — the forests of Karelia's national parks have never known an axe, thanks to impassable bogs. Some pines here are already more than five centuries old. Touch a living being that saw the sun before Ivan the Terrible did. In this untouched forest, you won't find a path for a hundred kilometers. And on the rare trails that exist, trees a couple of meters off the ground are marked with bear claws — so everyone knows who's in charge here.
